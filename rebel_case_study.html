About Rebel Athletic

Founded in 2012 and headquartered in the Dallas/Carrollton area, Rebel Athletic is a premium custom cheerleading and athletic apparel manufacturer serving competitive cheer programs, All-Star gyms, school teams, and dance organizations across the United States. In March 2025, Main Post Partners — a San Francisco-based private equity firm — made a minority investment, placing Rebel in active growth mode with expectations of technology-driven operational scaling.

Every Rebel order is essentially a unique SKU — custom color combinations, embellishments, athlete names, numbers, and sizing across an entire team roster. This extreme customization creates significant operational complexity at every stage: design approval, specification handoff to offshore manufacturers, production tracking, quality control, and final delivery ahead of competition deadlines that are fixed and unforgiving.

The company is currently mid-ERP transition, presenting a rare window to embed AI capabilities into new systems from the start rather than retrofitting legacy infrastructure. The PE firm expects quarterly AI milestone reporting, making this engagement both urgent and well-funded.

Key Problems Identified
1
Custom Order Specification Errors

22% of custom orders contain specification errors — wrong color codes, incorrect size runs, missing embellishment details — that aren't caught until the offshore factory produces the wrong item. Each remake costs $400–$800 per order and creates a cascading delivery delay. With thousands of orders per season, this single problem drives the majority of the company's rework costs.

2
Late Deliveries Before Competition Deadlines

34% of team orders arrive late relative to competition schedules. Unlike retail, cheer competitions are fixed-date events — a uniform arriving two days late means the team competes without it. Late deliveries trigger customer service escalations, refund demands, and lost renewals for the following season.

3
Offshore Supply Chain Visibility Gap

Once an order is handed off to offshore manufacturers, Rebel has limited real-time visibility into production status until the shipment arrives. This blind spot prevents proactive intervention when production falls behind and creates the surprise late deliveries that damage customer relationships.

4
Demand Planning Disconnected from Competition Calendar

Cheer competition seasons follow a highly predictable calendar — regional competitions in fall, nationals in winter/spring. Despite this predictability, production planning is reactive rather than proactive. Rush orders spike before major competitions, driving premium freight costs and factory overtime that compress margins.

Solution AI Solution Stack & Problem Mapping Page 02 / 04

DFW Technologies proposes a hybrid AI deployment for Rebel Athletic — private AI for order specifications, design IP, athlete personal data, and production system integration (operationally sensitive and customer IP), and public cloud AI for demand forecasting, customer service, and the coach-facing communication platform (no sensitive data involved). The ERP transition is the ideal integration entry point — embedding AI into new workflows from day one rather than retrofitting legacy systems.

Policies, Compliance & Restrictions to Address
Design IP & Order Data Privacy

Custom design files, color specifications, and embellishment details represent Rebel's proprietary product catalog and their clients' team identity. This data must stay in Rebel's private infrastructure — design IP must never be transmitted to public AI APIs or used to train shared models.

Athlete Personal Data (COPPA)

Cheer teams frequently include minor athletes under 13. Athlete names, numbers, sizes, and any personally identifiable information collected during order entry may be subject to COPPA protections. All athlete data must route exclusively to private AI infrastructure with strict access controls.

International Data Transfer (Offshore)

Production data transmitted to offshore manufacturing partners crosses international boundaries. Any AI integration connecting to offshore factory systems must comply with applicable data transfer regulations and ensure that customer order details are transmitted only via encrypted, authenticated channels.

GDPR — International Clients

Rebel serves international cheer organizations. European client data is subject to GDPR — consent management, right to erasure, and data portability requirements apply. The AI customer service platform must respect opt-out signals and data deletion requests from EU-based clients.

PE Reporting Requirements

Main Post Partners' investment creates a governance obligation — AI deployment milestones must be measurable and reportable on a quarterly basis. All AI workloads must have clear KPI instrumentation from day one so Rebel leadership can demonstrate technology ROI to the PE board.

AI Routing Decision

Order specifications, design IP, athlete data, and production system integration = private AI only. Demand forecasting (anonymized historical volumes + public calendar), customer service communications (order metadata only), and training platform = appropriate for public cloud AI.
