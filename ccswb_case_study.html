About CCSWB

Coca-Cola Southwest Beverages (CCSWB), a subsidiary of Arca Continental, is one of the largest Coca-Cola bottlers in the United States. Headquartered in Dallas, CCSWB produces, markets, and distributes Coca-Cola brand beverages across Texas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, and Arkansas — a four-state footprint that includes some of the fastest-growing metro regions in the country.

Operating 7 production plants and 37 sales and distribution facilities, CCSWB runs a "Total Beverage Company" model — covering everything from sparkling soft drinks and waters to teas, coffees, energy drinks, juices, and milk. This portfolio breadth adds significant planning complexity across procurement, manufacturing, and last-mile delivery.

With 9,000+ associates and a consumer base of 31 million, even marginal operational improvements translate to millions in annual savings — making CCSWB a compelling candidate for AI investment with clear, measurable ROI.

Key Problems Identified
1
Fragmented Demand Forecasting

37 distribution centers operate with disconnected demand planning. Seasonal spikes — summer peaks, holiday promotions, regional events — routinely cause over/understocking across the network, increasing waste and lost sales.

2
Reactive Production Line Maintenance

Bottling lines run sensitive equipment: fillers, cappers, pasteurizers. Without real-time sensor intelligence, failures are caught reactively. A single unplanned downtime event on a high-speed bottling line costs $50K–$150K in lost production.

3
Inefficient Fleet & Route Operations

Delivery operations across 37 DCs lack AI-assisted routing. Without dynamic re-routing based on traffic, weather, and store receiving windows, fuel costs and late-delivery penalties accumulate across the network.

4
Workforce Skill Gaps & Training Scale

9,000+ associates across 7 plants with no unified AI-assisted training platform. Line managers spend significant time on manual data gathering instead of coaching — a pattern validated by Invisible AI's deployments at Toyota, where root-cause analysis alone consumed 3–4 hours per day per line manager before AI intervention.

Solution AI Solution Stack & Problem Mapping Page 02 / 04

DFW Technologies proposes a hybrid AI deployment for CCSWB — private AI on-premises for production-sensitive operations (equipment data, quality control) and public cloud AI for logistics and demand planning where external data feeds are required. This routing decision is driven by data sensitivity, latency requirements, and total cost of ownership across each use case.

🏭
Predictive Maintenance — Bottling Lines
↳ Solves: Problem #2 — Reactive Maintenance
Private AI

Deploy IoT sensors on critical bottling line assets (fillers, cappers, pasteurizers) feeding a private on-premises AI anomaly detection model. AI establishes baseline equipment health signatures and flags deviations before they cause failures — shifting from run-to-failure to predict-and-prevent.

IIoT Sensors Edge ML SCADA Integration Anomaly Detection
📦
Demand Forecasting — 37 Distribution Centers
↳ Solves: Problem #1 — Fragmented Forecasting
Public AI

Cloud-based forecasting model connects historical sales data, seasonal calendars, regional event data, and weather signals to generate rolling 90-day demand forecasts per DC. No IP-sensitive data involved — external signals improve model accuracy and justify public cloud deployment.

Time-Series ML Cloud LLM APIs ERP Integration Demand Planning
🚛
Route Optimization & Fleet Intelligence
↳ Solves: Problem #3 — Fleet Inefficiency
Public AI

AI-powered route optimization integrating real-time traffic, weather, store receiving windows, and vehicle load data to dynamically re-sequence delivery runs. Fleet telematics layer adds temperature monitoring in-transit for cold-chain compliance. Reduces miles, fuel, and late-delivery fees.

Route Optimization AI Telematics API Cold-Chain IoT Last-Mile Analytics

Computer Vision Quality Inspection
↳ Solves: Problem #2 (secondary) — Quality Control
Private AI

Edge-deployed computer vision on bottling lines detects label misalignment, fill-level deviations, and cap seal defects in real time — at line speed. Reduces manual inspection labor and catches defects before products leave the plant. Deployed on-premises for latency and IP reasons.

Industry Benchmark: Invisible AI — Active Platform, Deployed at Toyota, Ford & Aerospace Manufacturers

Invisible AI's edge-based computer vision platform — deployed across all 14 Toyota Motor North America manufacturing plants — is the leading active reference for this solution class. Using self-contained edge AI devices (NVIDIA Jetson chipset, 3D depth cameras, up to 4TB on-device storage), it processes assembly line video entirely on-premises with no cloud dependency. Toyota reports root-cause analysis reduced from 3–4 hours to minutes; manufacturers cite 3–5x ROI per device in efficiency gains alone, with every minute of downtime prevented saving $1,000. DFW Technologies applies this same edge-AI + computer vision methodology to CCSWB's bottling operations — adapting the approach from automotive assembly to beverage manufacturing at SME scale, without the enterprise price tag.

Policies, Compliance & Restrictions to Address
FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA)

AI quality inspection systems must support FSMA traceability requirements. All defect detection logs must be audit-ready and retained per FDA guidelines.

Arca Continental Data Governance

As an Arca Continental subsidiary, CCSWB's production and sales data is subject to parent company data governance policies. Any cloud-connected system must align with Arca's approved vendor list and data residency requirements.

NLRA & Union Considerations

AI-based worker monitoring (including training performance tracking) must comply with National Labor Relations Act provisions. Worker data usage in AI systems requires transparent communication and potentially union consultation at unionized plants.

DOT Fleet Compliance

Fleet telematics and AI routing tools must align with DOT hours-of-service regulations. Driver data collected through telematics is subject to ELD mandate requirements.

Cold Chain Temperature Logs

Temperature monitoring IoT data must be retained and auditable per Coca-Cola brand standards for beverage quality. Any cold-chain breach must trigger documented incident response.

Data Routing Decision

Production line sensor data and equipment health data must remain on-premises (private AI). Demand forecasting and routing data may use public cloud services where no production IP is involved.

Architecture Hybrid AI Architecture — CCSWB Deployment Page 04 / 04

The architecture follows a two-lane AI routing model: sensitive operational data (equipment sensors, quality inspection) stays on-premises behind private AI nodes; logistics and demand data flows through a secure cloud layer. A central API gateway manages data exchange between the two environments without exposing production IP.
