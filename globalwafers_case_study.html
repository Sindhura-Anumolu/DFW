About GlobalWafers America

GlobalWafers Co., Ltd. — headquartered in Hsinchu, Taiwan — is one of the world's top three semiconductor silicon wafer manufacturers. In 2022, it selected Sherman, Texas for a $5B+ greenfield investment, constructing the first 300mm silicon wafer fab to be built in the United States in over 20 years. The Sherman campus spans 142 acres and is designed for up to six production phases.

Phase 1 is now operational, producing 600,000 wafers per month. Silicon wafers are the foundational substrate for every advanced semiconductor — chips for electric vehicles, AI accelerators, consumer electronics, and defense systems all begin here. The facility is the only advanced 300mm silicon wafer fab currently operating in the U.S., making it strategically critical to domestic chip supply chain resilience.

GlobalWafers received $406M in direct CHIPS and Science Act funding, with additional funding tied to workforce development requirements. Phase 2 construction is imminent, with plans to hire up to 650 engineering, technical, and operational employees by 2028 — largely from a Texas workforce with no pre-existing semiconductor manufacturing experience.

Key Problems Identified
1
Workforce Ramp-Up Crisis — No Local Talent Pipeline

Sherman has no semiconductor manufacturing history. GlobalWafers must hire and train 650+ technicians and engineers by 2028 for roles that require specialized cleanroom skills not available through traditional hiring. The U.S. semiconductor industry is projected to face a shortage of 70,000–90,000 fab workers when new fabs come online — GlobalWafers is at the center of this crisis.

2
Knowledge Transfer from Taiwan Operations

Decades of process expertise, equipment troubleshooting procedures, and yield optimization knowledge exist in the heads of GlobalWafers' Taiwan engineering team. That knowledge must be systematically captured and transferred to a new U.S. workforce — before Taiwan engineers rotate out of Sherman.

3
Cleanroom Sensor Monitoring & Yield Protection

Silicon wafer manufacturing tolerates defects at parts-per-million levels. Temperature, humidity, vibration, and airborne particle counts inside cleanrooms must be monitored continuously. Any deviation — even micro-vibrations — causes yield loss worth tens of thousands of dollars per affected batch.

4
Equipment Maintenance at Multi-Million Dollar Scale

Each cleanroom tool (deposition systems, polishers, inspection equipment) is worth $2M–$20M. Reactive maintenance is not an option. Without predictive analytics on equipment sensor data, unplanned failures cost production time and risk contaminating entire cleanroom batches.

⚠️
Critical Note: All AI for GlobalWafers must be deployed private / on-premises only. No process data, yield data, equipment parameters, or SOP content may touch a public cloud service — this is both IP policy and CHIPS Act compliance. Public cloud is not an option for any workload at this facility.

Policies, Compliance & Restrictions to Address
CHIPS & Science Act — Data Governance

GlobalWafers' $406M federal award includes data security obligations. Process data, yield records, and equipment parameters must remain within approved data perimeters. Any AI system touching this data must be on-premises and auditable per Commerce Department requirements.

Trade Secret & IP Protection

Silicon wafer process recipes and yield optimization parameters are among the most closely guarded IP in manufacturing. No process data may be transmitted to public cloud AI services. All models must be trained and run on GlobalWafers-controlled infrastructure.

Export Control (EAR / ITAR Adjacency)

Semiconductor manufacturing equipment and processes may be subject to Export Administration Regulations. AI systems that capture and store process knowledge must be access-controlled to prevent unauthorized disclosure to foreign nationals, consistent with EAR requirements.

CHIPS Workforce Development Obligations

Federal funding includes a $6M CHIPS workforce development allocation with specific reporting requirements. AI training platform must generate audit-ready records of training completion, competency assessments, and workforce development outcomes to satisfy federal reporting.

ISO 14644 Cleanroom Standards

Cleanroom monitoring AI must align with ISO 14644 classifications for particle counts, temperature, and humidity. Alert thresholds and monitoring intervals must meet or exceed the cleanroom class requirements for 300mm wafer fabrication environments.

Texas Labor & Employment Law

AI-based skill tracking and competency gating systems that affect employment decisions must comply with Texas employment law and any applicable federal anti-discrimination regulations. Transparent documentation of how AI assessments are used in employment decisions is required.

Architecture Private-Only AI Architecture — GlobalWafers Deployment Page 04 / 04

GlobalWafers uses a fully private, air-gapped AI architecture. All data — sensor streams, process records, knowledge base, training content, yield data — stays within GlobalWafers' on-premises network perimeter. No external APIs, no cloud inference, no public services. Three internal zones: Production Floor (sensors & equipment), AI Processing Layer (models & analytics), and User Interface Layer (dashboards & training) — all interconnected via secured internal network only.
