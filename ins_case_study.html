About INS

Founded in 1998 and headquartered in Richardson, Texas, Industrial Networking Solutions (INS) delivers industrial networking products, engineering services, and Operational Technology (OT) managed services to clients in manufacturing, food and beverage, oil and gas, transportation, and utilities. With approximately 91 employees, INS competes as a specialist against larger IT generalists by offering deep OT domain expertise and hands-on engineering.

INS has evolved from Industrial Ethernet to M2M to the Industrial Internet of Things — now offering private cellular (LTE/5G), rugged networking hardware, OT cybersecurity, edge computing deployment, and network lifecycle management. Their highest-value asset is deep OT network engineering expertise concentrated in a small number of senior engineers — a significant scaling constraint as client complexity grows.

INS approached DFW Technologies directly about digital twin solutions for their oil and gas clients — making this the warmest lead in the six-company portfolio. The problem is pre-identified, the relationship is established, and the ask maps directly to DFW Tech's capabilities.

Policies, Compliance & Restrictions to Address
Client OT Network Data — Absolute Privacy

Client network topology, device inventories, IP addressing schemes, and configuration files are operationally critical and competitively sensitive. This data must never leave INS-owned or client-premises infrastructure. No network topology data may be transmitted to any public API under any circumstance.

NERC CIP / ICS Security Standards

Clients in utilities and energy sectors may be subject to NERC CIP standards governing OT cybersecurity. Any AI-based threat detection or monitoring system deployed for these clients must align with NERC CIP compliance requirements and not create new audit exposure for the client.

CMMC & HIPAA Client Exposure

INS serves clients in defense supply chain (CMMC) and healthcare-adjacent industries (HIPAA). AI monitoring tools must be architecturally separated per client — shared infrastructure between clients with different compliance regimes creates cross-contamination risk and audit exposure.

Incident Response Legal Obligations

When AI threat detection identifies a security incident, INS may have contractual or legal obligations to notify clients within specific timeframes. AI-generated alerts must feed into a human-reviewed incident response workflow — AI does not autonomously notify clients or take network action.

Pre-Sales Assessment Data Handling

The AI pre-sales readiness tool collects client infrastructure metadata. Even at a high level, this data represents competitive intelligence about a prospect's OT environment. Data collected during pre-sales assessments must be handled under NDA and not used to train shared AI models.

AI Routing Decision

All client network data workloads (threat detection, knowledge copilot, digital twin backend, churn model) are private AI only. Pre-sales assessment and client health reporting use only aggregated metadata and are appropriate for managed public cloud — no client-identifiable data transmitted.
